About

ICSEET 2025 will provide an excellent international forum to engineers and scientists from both industry and academia to present their ongoing work, relate their research outcomes and experiences, and discuss the best and most efficient Software Engineering practices to improve the development process by integrating the emerging technologies such as Internet of Things, Quantum Computing (QC), Cloud Computing (CC), Big data analytics and so on. Moreover, It also provides an excellent opportunity for the academic community to become more aware of subject areas critical to the software industry as practitioners bring their needs to the table. The 1st ICSEET conference will be held from August 11-12, 2025 in Long Beach, California, USA.

Topics Of Interests

· Software Engineering for Quantum Computing Application · Software Engineering for Internet of Things · Evidence-Based Software Engineering · Search-Based Software Engineering · Emerging Technologies Integration in Software Development · Effort and Cost Measurement through Metrics · Process and Quantity Issues. · Software Fault Prediction · Software Maintenance and Evolution · Fairness in Software Testing · Formal Methods · Requirement Engineering · Security and Privacy Concerns · LLM-Based Software Engineering · Generative AI for software development

Tracks

Regular Long Paper

Submit original manuscripts (not published or considered elsewhere) with a maximum of twelve pages (context and references). Each paper should include a title, the name and affiliation of each author, a 300-word abstract, and up to 6 keywords. The format of your submission must follow the ICSEET conference proceedings format (PDF | Word DOCX | Latex).

Regular Short paper

The objective of short papers is to report novel ideas and results that have not been fully evaluated. Submit original manuscripts (not published or considered elsewhere) with a maximum of ten pages (context and references). Each paper should include a title, the name and affiliation of each author, a 300-word abstract, and up to 6 keywords. The format of your submission must follow the QRS conference proceedings format (PDF | Word DOCX | Latex).

Student Research Competition

The Student Research Competition (SRC) offers a unique forum for undergraduate and graduate students to present their original research before a panel of judges and attendees at conference. The SRC track provides participants a chance to meet other students and to get direct feedback on their work from experts. The steps to participate in SRC as follow.

  • Submission through the system
  • SRC article could be submitted by undergraduate and graduate level students with their supervisor names.
  • First day, the author of accepted articles will join the poster session where a team of judges will select the top 5 articles.
  • Second day, authors of selected article will do oral presentation and top three authors will get cash reward of 400$, 300$ and 200$ respectively.
Each paper should include a title, the name and affiliation of each author, a 300-word abstract, and up to 6 keywords. The format of your submission must follow the QRS conference proceedings format (PDF | Word DOCX | Latex).Submission
